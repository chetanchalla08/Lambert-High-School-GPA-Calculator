How to Use the GPA Calculator

This guide will help you understand how to use the GPA Calculator to accurately calculate your academic performance.

Getting Started

Begin by visiting the GPA Calculator web page. You will see a simple interface with options to input your course grades and select the course type.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Add Courses: Click the 'Add Course' button to start entering your course details. For each course, you need to input the percentage grade and select the class type (Normal, AP, Dual Enrollment).
  2. Calculate GPA: Once all courses have been added, click the 'Calculate GPA' button to see your unweighted and weighted GPA.

Tips for Accurate Calculations

  • Ensure all percentage grades are entered accurately.
  • Correctly classify each course as Normal, AP, or Dual Enrollment for an accurate weighted GPA.
  • Include all academic and summer courses for comprehensive GPA calculation.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I calculate GPA for less than 4 years?

Yes, you can calculate your GPA for any number of years, from 1 to 4. Simply add the courses relevant to your calculation.

How are AP and Dual Enrollment courses weighted?

AP and Dual Enrollment courses are given an additional point towards your GPA calculation, reflecting their increased difficulty compared to regular courses.

What if I make a mistake in my course entry?

If you enter a course incorrectly, you can always add another course with the correct information. After that, you can remove the course that you entered the wrong information for.
